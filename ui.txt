Design and build the UI for Meridian, a blogging platform built exclusively for engineers and tech enthusiasts. Unlike algorithmic, follower-driven platforms, Meridian's identity rests on: stack-matched content discovery, posts that can be patched and kept accurate over time ("Living Posts"), GitHub-style forking of posts with attribution, impact-based pay (paid when a reader bookmarks, shares, or marks a post "used this at work" — not per pageview), inline claim citations with community verification, a structured Q&A panel that becomes an auto-generated FAQ, an auto-built public skills profile from published writing, and a mentored publishing track for junior engineers.
Imagine a stunning, visually rich and animated landing page. Instead of static content, a captivating background animation unfolds. The theme is "Knowledge Unfolding and Impact Rippling." A stylized, glowing network of interconnected nodes and lines gradually builds across the screen, starting small and blossoming into a vast, intricate web, symbolizing the community's collective knowledge and the "Stack-Matched Content Discovery."
As a user scrolls or after a key message is delivered, a different sequence begins. Multiple smaller data patterns converge and flow into a single, larger node. This visual metaphor represents "GitHub-style Forking with Attribution" and the flow of ideas. Simultaneously, ripples of light and a cascading display of stylized currency symbols (or impact tokens) emanating from prominent nodes, visually interpreting the "Impact-Based Pay" model.
The text on the landing page will be minimal, powerful, and fade in smoothly as the animation progresses. Prominent, styled buttons ("Join Meridian," "Learn More") with a subtle, pulsating hover effect are positioned for easy access. The entire landing page should feel fluid, dynamic, and reflect the innovation at the core of Meridian.
Design a minimal top navigation bar between the left sidebar and right side-panel for a modern developer-focused platform. On the left, display a bold heading "Discover" in black text. In the center, place a compact rounded search bar with a subtle light-gray border, a search icon on the left, and placeholder text "Search stack..." in muted gray. On the right, include two small monochrome icons: a settings gear and an information/help icon. Use a clean white background, thin gray bottom border, ample horizontal spacing, minimalist styling, and an overall GitHub-inspired aesthetic. Height should be around 48px, with a professional, lightweight, responsive design suitable for a SaaS dashboard.
Overall Design Aesthetics (Refined for a Touch of Colour):
Let's maintain the sophisticated, monochrome-forward feel, but with a vibrant and meaningful application of color for essential information.
•	Primary Palette:
o	--color-primary: #000000 (true black) — for primary text, icons, essential buttons, active navigation states, selected tabs.
o	--color-surface: #EAECEC — for page background, card background elements, hairline dividers, hover states.
o	--color-muted: #999B9B — for secondary/tertiary text, placeholders, inactive icons, disabled states.
•	Semantic Accent Palette (The Subtle Colour Infusion):
o	--color-verified: #00C896 (Teal) — Used exclusively for "Verified Claim" badges, the main verification icon, and primary call-to-action buttons (like "Join Meridian" or "Confirm Contribution").
o	--color-flagged: #FF6B6B (Coral) — Used only for "Unverified Claim" flags and major warnings (e.g., in Patch Review).
o	--color-highlight: #FFB900 (Amber) — Used sparingly for temporary notifications, specific action highlights (e.g., when content is forked), and to draw attention without overpowering.
